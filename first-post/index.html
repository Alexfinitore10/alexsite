Decisions Decisions

Alex August 21, 2025

As a first post I would like to discuss about me and my passions

I really spend a lot of time on my computer. It started when I bought one at 13 years old, having little prior experience with them on the family computer I had in the living room. The first PC was the first big decision of my life, that shaped what I would be in the next future. Obviously it came to me in a time where the fine line between using and abusing was not really well defined in my mind, and the "administrators" of that fine line didn't care. So I stayed as long as I wanted, but that is something that must be discussed in a different topic.
By trying and trying something I actually went from a complete garbage user to a mild understander of the concepts of "using the pc". Obviously, I'm no gifted person, so I stayed and became familliar with the user experience that I always had : Windows, not learning at all deeply about computers, just rather using it for the main reasons a kid would have to use it. Youtube, videogames and chatting.

And it went like this, for all of the high school years, giving it all up to the videogame scene. And puff, at the end of it, just when I had to decide what I wanted to persue as a career, I chose Computer Science. As of today, not the smartest decision, but the most sensed one. I have spent litterally all the most important school years on a computer, so it had to be it, right?

Fast forward to now... It was a difficult path, full of mistakes, injustice, wrath but a little of more experience on my side.Â 

My expectations were completely wiped up as I continued the course and my passion of it started shaking. With it, the newly discovered "disease" or "problem" to my eyes was just presenting itself, giving me a really tough time with studying, psychocologically.

Passions huh? So It started with videogames, obviously, I really wanted to become one of the people behind the courtain, and not just frontally playing it. It was a beautiful past time, fantasizing about what I wanted to make and what I had to learn to make it and facing the harsh reality. I even followed some tutorials on making some, but with poor results.

Having facing that, I went to the simplest of the game engine, RPG Maker, and made "the first game" with a friend. The development time was beautiful, full of passion, creativity and a lot of laughs. Surely some well spent time, I must say. A time that I will always remember. After the release of it the ideas started blooming and pumping from a simpler game to much difficult and time consuming ideas. The bases were told and formed but, somehow and something dragged me out of that, so I backed up of the project, leaving my friend doing it solo, and that, eventually, led him to back up too.

Making videogames is hard. I didn't have any weapons with me at that time. Now I actually have, but I don't feel like doing anything games related, even tho, it's a pretty dope idea in my mind.
The 5 years of university were permeated with the idea that I had time for making a new game, but I never actually went serious on anything. There was just a moment during the Covid pandemic where I decided to do something on my own, It actually went a little ok, but then I folded the project. It was a pretty basic one.

As of today, videogame making is a scraped idea on my mind. The market is pretty full and layoffs is the current fashion. In a place like that, I would never go, it would be a bad experience, I know. So I went to look for something that I would like more than other things in CS. And then I found Networks.

I always had network problems at my home, problems that fortunately would solve because of the fact that my father worked with a private internet company and the issues would soon be solved, fortunately. But also with that, the internet was really really bad for the standard of the day. So I always went and investigate what the problem would be, but with little or no results. That led me to get interested in networks.

Computer networks is a topic of further topics, that I would like to talk more and more.
